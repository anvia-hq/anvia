# Code of Conduct

Anvia should be a respectful, practical, and inclusive project space. This code of conduct applies to project repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, community spaces, and events.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Treat others with respect and patience.
- Assume good intent while still being clear about technical concerns.
- Give feedback that is specific, actionable, and focused on the work.
- Accept constructive feedback without personal attacks.
- Respect different levels of experience and different communication styles.
- Credit others for their work and ideas.
- Keep private information private.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, slurs, demeaning language, or personal attacks.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or sexualized language.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussion after maintainers redirect it.
- Bad-faith reports, trolling, or intentionally wasting maintainer time.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make someone feel unsafe or unwelcome.

## Technical Discussion

Strong technical disagreement is allowed. Personal hostility is not.

Good disagreement focuses on:

- Reproducible behavior.
- Code, tests, docs, and public API impact.
- Tradeoffs, compatibility, maintenance cost, and user outcomes.

Poor disagreement targets a person, their motives, background, or ability.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action when behavior violates this code of conduct. Actions may include:

- A private reminder or warning.
- Editing or deleting comments.
- Locking or closing issues and pull requests.
- Temporary or permanent loss of participation privileges.
- Escalation to platform moderation when needed.

Maintainers will aim to apply this code consistently and proportionately.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers privately. Include:

- What happened.
- Where and when it happened.
- Links, screenshots, or logs when available.
- Whether there is an immediate safety or privacy concern.

Reports will be handled with care. Maintainers will avoid sharing reporter information beyond what is needed to review and respond to the report.

## Scope

This code applies when participating in Anvia spaces and when representing the project elsewhere. It also applies when behavior outside project spaces materially affects the safety or trust of project participants.
